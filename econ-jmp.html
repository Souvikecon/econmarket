⭑ Editor's Pick — Labor Economics
"Monopsony Power and Wage Determination in Local Labor Markets: Evidence from Matched Employer-Employee Data"
Clara Voss — Ph.D. Candidate, MIT Economics
This paper identifies the causal effect of employer market power on wages using novel matched employer-employee data from German administrative records spanning 1995–2022. Exploiting plant closures as exogenous shocks to local labor supply, I estimate that a one standard-deviation increase in the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index reduces wages by 7.3 percent, with effects concentrated among non-college workers and manufacturing sectors.
