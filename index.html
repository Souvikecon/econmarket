⭑ Editor's Pick — Development Economics · 2025 NEUDC Distinguished Paper Award
"Depreciation and Growth: Evidence from Machine Repair in Uganda"
Tilman Graff — Ph.D. Candidate, Harvard University (Advisors: Breza, Kreindler, Gabaix, Straub)
Capital depreciation restricts economic growth, yet little is known about whether it differs in developing countries and why. This paper studies the market for machine repair in Uganda using detailed field data and a spatial growth model with endogenous depreciation. Machinery upkeep costs are around twice as high as in the US, throttling investment — particularly for small and remote firms. A calibrated growth model accounts for 7–9% of income differences across Ugandan regions.

About EconMarket

EconMarket is an independent, free-to-use directory of economics job market papers. Our mission is to make the economics job market more transparent and accessible — for candidates, hiring committees, and the broader research community.

Each year we list job market papers from PhD candidates at universities worldwide, organized by field and searchable by keyword, author, and institution. Our placement tracker follows candidates through the market season, providing the field with data on outcomes across institution types and subfields. The 2025–26 season features candidates from MIT, Harvard, Chicago, Princeton, Georgetown, BU, Brown, and many more top programs.

EconMarket is not affiliated with the American Economic Association, NBER, or any university. Candidate data is sourced directly from departmental placement pages. If you are a candidate and wish to update your listing, please use the submission form.

How It Works

Candidates submit their paper directly through our submission form. Each submission is reviewed by an editor with expertise in the relevant field before being published. Listings include the full abstract, author contact information, field classification, and a link to the full paper. Candidates can update their listing at any time and mark themselves as placed once they accept an offer.

Hiring committees and researchers can browse freely, filter by field, and contact candidates directly. We do not charge any fee for either side of the market.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can submit a paper?
Any PhD student currently on the economics job market is welcome to submit. Papers are typically submitted in September–November of the job market year. We accept papers in all subfields of economics, finance, and quantitative social science.
How long does review take?
Most submissions are reviewed within 2–3 business days. During peak submission season (October–November) it may take up to one week. You'll receive an email notification when your listing goes live.
Can I update my listing after submitting?
Yes. You'll receive an edit link via email. You can update your abstract, paper URL, contact preferences, and placement status at any time throughout the season.
How is placement data collected?
We ask candidates to self-report their placement outcome once they accept an offer. We also track publicly announced placements from department websites and X/Twitter. All placement data is verified before being added to the tracker.
Is EconMarket free?
Yes, completely free for candidates and employers. EconMarket is supported by voluntary institutional sponsorships from economics departments who want to support a transparent job market.
